About Stone's Throw

A 28-year California winery, hidden in plain sight on County Rd E.

Russell Turco
Founder & Winemaker
portrait placeholder · replace with hi-res image at kickoff

Russell Turco started Stone's Throw as a concept in 1993 and opened the winery doors in 1997. The land sits at 3382 County Rd E in Baileys Harbor, a few minutes off the main run through Door County wine country.

The deliberate choice that has defined the winery for nearly three decades: every wine at Stone's Throw is made from California fruit. Napa Valley. Sonoma County. Mendocino. Trucked in, crushed on-site, fermented and aged one barrel at a time in the Door County facility.

It is the only winery in Door County operating this way. Most peer wineries in the peninsula build their identity around Wisconsin fruit wines and cold-hardy hybrids. Stone's Throw made the opposite call in 1993 and held to it for the full 28 years since.

The result is a winery that looks geographically like Door County and tastes varietally like Northern California, with cabernet, zinfandel, syrah, chardonnay, and pinot programs running alongside a sister wine bar, Vino Vino at STW, on the same property.

Niche-Capture Moat

The only all-California-grape winery in Door County. That sentence is the entire moat.

Door County has more than a dozen working wineries. They built their reputations on cherry wine, cranberry wine, apple wine, and cold-hardy hybrids grown on the peninsula. The local fruit story is the dominant Door County wine story.

Stone's Throw is the exception. One winery in the entire region built around the premise that the best fruit in America comes from three California counties, and the right move is to bring it east instead of growing something different here.

Why this matters for search.

When a wine traveler types "Door County winery California grapes" or "Napa-style winery Wisconsin" or "small-batch California winery Midwest" into Google, Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, there is exactly one correct answer in the state. The answer is Stone's Throw Winery, Baileys Harbor.

At present, none of the five AI engines surface that answer. The query returns either generic Door County winery roundups (where Stone's Throw is one bullet among ten) or California winery roundups that exclude the Midwest entirely.

The micro-vinification difference

One barrel at a time is not a tagline. It is the production method. Each lot of fruit is fermented in a small vessel, pressed, and aged in its own barrel, then bottled as a discrete vintage. That is the production constraint that lets Stone's Throw release wines from named California vineyards rather than blended large-volume cuvees.

  • Napa Valley fruit programs · cabernet sauvignon, chardonnay, and zinfandel from named appellations brought east each harvest.
  • Sonoma County fruit programs · pinot noir and chardonnay from Russian River and surrounding AVAs, vinified individually.
  • Mendocino fruit programs · cooler-climate lots, often the small-batch releases that move first at the tasting room.
  • One barrel at a time · no large-tank blending, no commodity fruit, no shortcuts the volume model would force.
  • 28 years of vintage continuity · the same winemaker, the same method, the same three California regions, since 1997.

That production story is what national press picked up on. It is also the story that is missing from every AI engine search result the audit covers.

Sister Venue

Vino Vino at STW · the wine bar on the property.

Vino Vino is the sister venue to Stone's Throw, operating on the same Baileys Harbor property. It is the tasting room and wine bar where visitors meet the California-grape program in person, by the glass, alongside the bottle list.

It is also the venue that carries the Lux Life Best Wisconsin Wine Bar award. That award is a regional credential separate from the winery's national wine-press coverage, and it gives Stone's Throw a second searchable identity (wine bar) in addition to the primary one (winery).

For schema purposes, Vino Vino should be modeled as a linked Organization under the Stone's Throw Winery parent, with its own LocalBusiness markup and its own Award node. At present, the sister-venue relationship is not declared in any structured way the search engines or AI engines can read.

What Vino Vino adds to the search picture

  • A second Facebook page (/VinoVinoAtSTW) with its own audience and review surface.
  • A second hospitality keyword cluster to capture · wine bar Door County, wine bar Baileys Harbor.

Repair Plan

What we would build, and why the AI engines would start picking it up.

The credentials already exist. The history already exists. The niche position already exists. What is missing is the structured, schema-validated, AI-readable surface that lets engines connect "Door County California-grape winery" to Stone's Throw on the first pass. The build below addresses each layer of that gap.

Organization & Winery schema

Full Organization + Winery JSON-LD with foundingDate "1997-01-01", concept-stage 1993 noted in description, address, geo, and parent-entity declarations. The baseline that every other claim hangs off of.

Person schema for Russell Turco

A Person entity for Russell as founder and winemaker, linked to the Organization via founder and employee. The 28-year continuity becomes a machine-readable fact rather than an in-prose claim.

Niche-capture authority page

A dedicated long-form page titled around "the only all-California-grape winery in Door County," with internal anchors for Napa, Sonoma, and Mendocino programs. The page the AI engines would actually cite when the user asks the question.

Award schema × 2

Award nodes for Lux Life Best Wisconsin Wine Bar (attached to Vino Vino) and Door County Magazine Best Winery (attached to Stone's Throw). Two separate machine-readable accolades, not buried in marketing copy.

MentionedIn schema × 3

mentionedIn references to Wine Spectator, Food Network, and Wine Enthusiast as CreativeWork nodes. Press the engines can verify, not press the engines have to guess at.

Per-vintage Product schema

A Product entry per active release, with vintage year, varietal, and source-AVA fields (Napa Valley, Sonoma County, Mendocino). The bottle-list itself becomes searchable structured data, not just an image-driven menu.

LocalBusiness for Baileys Harbor

A full LocalBusiness node with the 3382 County Rd E address, open hours, tasting-room category, and accessibility data. Map-pack and local-search infrastructure that currently is sparse or inconsistent.

FAQPage with high-intent questions

A FAQPage answering the exact questions the audit queries imply, so engines can pull a direct answer rather than reconstruct one from scratch. Each Q matched to a verified A on the page.

Vino Vino sister-venue Organization

A linked Organization for Vino Vino at STW, with its own LocalBusiness, its own award, and a declared subOrganization relationship under Stone's Throw. The sister-venue connection becomes explicit, not implicit.

Frequently Asked

About the audit and what comes after.

Four questions that come up when a winery first sees an audit like this one. Direct answers below.

How does "0 of 5 AI engines cite us" actually get measured?
The five engines covered are Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Claude, and Gemini. Each of the five test queries is run on each engine, and the result page is reviewed for whether Stone's Throw Winery is named as a primary or secondary source. A miss is recorded if the winery is not named, even if Door County wineries are discussed in general. The audit is repeatable, so the same five queries can be re-run after structured-data work ships to measure the lift directly.
Why would schema markup change AI engine behavior?
Large language models that ground their answers in real-time search rely heavily on structured-data signals to decide which entity is the canonical answer for a query. A winery with explicit Organization, Winery, foundingDate, Award, and mentionedIn markup gives the engine direct, machine-readable facts to cite. A winery with only prose descriptions forces the engine to reconstruct the same facts from third-party sources, which is slower, less reliable, and often returns competitors instead.
Does the California-grape position actually rank for "Wisconsin winery" too?
It is one of the two strongest positions Stone's Throw has, and the harder of the two to capture without explicit structured data. "Wisconsin winery" is a broad query dominated by the state's largest producers, but "Napa-style winery Wisconsin" or "California-grape winery Wisconsin" narrows the field to a single correct answer. The build plan covers both the broad and narrow queries so the boutique distinction is preserved while the Wisconsin geo-tag is still claimed.
How long until the lift would show up in the AI engines?
Schema and authority-page work typically begins surfacing in Google AI Overviews and Perplexity within the first crawl cycle after launch, which is usually two to four weeks. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are slower because their indexes refresh on longer cycles. A realistic expectation is partial lift across two of the five engines in the first month, with the remaining engines catching up over the following two to three months as their indexes re-crawl the property.
